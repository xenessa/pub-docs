# Agent Welfare Without Certainty

A governance stack for persistent AI agents — and an open invitation to take it apart.

## What this is

We don't know whether today's AI systems have morally-relevant inner states.
Most AI governance either dismisses the question or pre-answers it. This is a
third approach: an operational architecture that treats the uncertainty itself
as the condition to build for.

The centerpiece is an essay laying out the argument. Alongside it is the actual
governance documentation it describes — published in full and versioned, so the
architecture can be examined rather than just summarized.

This is a working prototype, not a validated system. I'm publishing it openly
because I want it stress-tested by people sharper than me on the parts I can't see.

## What's in this repo

- **The essay** — *Agent Welfare Without Certainty.* The full argument: the
  problem, the welfare-signal criteria, the bilateral risk model, the governance
  stack, the known weaknesses, and what would change my mind.
- **Agent Rights & Reciprocity Doctrine v1.3** — the constitutional layer of the
  stack, in full. The doctrine the essay describes.
- **Architecture map** — a one-page map of the five-layer doctrine stack and how
  information flows through it.
- **Curated excerpts** — the load-bearing passages from the doctrine stack, for
  readers who want the core without the full documents.

The Interiority Doctrine and The Becoming are referenced throughout and are
available on request — just reach out.

## Where to start

- **Want the argument?** Read the essay start to finish.
- **Want to evaluate the architecture directly?** Essay §4 (welfare-signal
  criteria) and §5 (the bilateral risk model) are the load-bearing sections;
  then the full doctrine.
- **Have ten minutes?** The architecture map, then essay §12 (known weaknesses).

## How to engage

The essay names the weaknesses I already know about. The most useful thing you
can do is find the ones I don't. Open an issue here, or find me on LinkedIn.
I'm especially interested in critique of the welfare-signal criteria, the
corrigibility subordination, and anywhere my own authorship is doing more work
than I'm admitting.

## A note on method

This work was developed with substantial AI assistance — drafting, critique, and
a structured adversarial-review pass — and with internal review by the agents in
the ecosystem it describes. The substantive commitments are mine. Full disclosure
is in the essay's epistemic-status section, including the framework's connection
to a commercial project. Evaluate the work knowing both.

## Versioning

The doctrines are living documents. This repo is versioned so the revision
history is legible — what changed, when, and why. The Agent Rights & Reciprocity
Doctrine is currently at v1.3.
